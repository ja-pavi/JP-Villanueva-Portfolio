CS 373 Fall 2023: Jacob Villanueva

Blog #4 for Software Engineering Fall 2023

By Jacob Villanueva

September 17th, 2023

What did you do this past week?

I'm writing this while sitting in the San Jose airport after a weekend trip to California. I was fortunate enough to be able to attend the Hispanic Scholarship Fund (HSF) Stem Summit in San Jose, CA as a HSF Scholar these past couple of days. I was able to meet some great people, now turned friends, as well as talk to alumni of the program as well on career opportunities and mentoring. Overall, it was a great experience, though I find myself both eager and ready to return to Austin.

What's in your way?

Currently, I don't believe there is anything in my way. Though there is a opportunity in front of me, that I'm looking forward to acting on at the beginning of this week.

What will you do next week?

I'm looking forward to working on my second individual project for Game Technology, catching up on a couple of courses, and coordinating with my team on Assignment #2 for the course I am writing this blog for, CS 373.

What did you think of Paper #4: All I Really Need to Know about Pair Programming I Learned In Kindergarten?

It's always fun to re-explore ideas that you have visited in the past. In this situation, I am revisiting the idea of pair programming and the skills need it to do it successfully. I was first introduced to the idea of pair programming in CS 314, Data Structures. Here, we spent time working on a couple of different projects with a partner, and had to be able manager our code base successfully as a team. I was happy to revisit the topic in the paper annotations.

What was your experience of the IDB1 project, now that it's starting? (This question will vary, week to week.)

I'm happy with the teammates I got, as everyone has been pretty easy to communicate with. Though we haven't collaborated too much yet due to a couple of us being busy over the weekend, I greatly look forward to connecting with the gang this upcoming week. Exciting stuff is going to come from this group.

What made you happy this week?

Making new friends at the Summit, I hope to see them again in the future.

What's your pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

Pick-of-the-week has to be GDExstension, a Godot plugin, that allows for C++ script to be ran inside the Godot Mono game engine.
