CS 373 Fall 2023: Jacob Villanueva

Blog #1 for Software Engineering Fall 2023

By Jacob Villanueva

August 26th, 2023

Where did you grow up?

I grew up just north of Houston in Spring, Texas.

What high school did you attend?

I attended Klein High School.

Why did you come to UT?

As a Texas kid, being able to attend the best public school in the state and the largest college brand in the nation was an easy choice. With UT having a great business school, great computer science program, the value was unbeatable for in-state tuition. I got into some strong out-of-state programs, but I never wanted to leave Texas.

What was your favorite extracurricular activity in high school?

My favorite highschool extracurricular activity in high school was attending local hackathons with close friends. In my senior year, we built a geo-fencing location system for elementary school parent pick up systems and got first in multiple categories of the competition. I made life long friends through this, as well as learned many skills through building ideas that interested me.

Why are you majoring in CS?

I'm majoring in CS because I believe that it allows me to technical software skills that can be applied to any industry. Through my degree I have been able to explore a myriad of career paths and many industries. As technology continues to evolve, I believe that a programming background is only going to get more and more important!

Why are you in this class?

I'm in this class as I want to expand my skillset around Python, SQL, and collaborating with peers. I have heard this course be recommended by many upperclassmen as well.

What are your expectations of this class?

My expectations surround coming out with a sufficient foundation around Python and SQL, while also understanding the collaboration that goes on within the software engineering industry.

How much do you know about Javascript/Python/SQL/Web programming?

I have explored and used all 4 of these tools, however I have very little formal education in them. I taught myself how to use the basics of them through youtube videos and building out passion projects.

How did you like the first lectures?

I was not on campus for the first lecture, however the third lecture was great. I enjoyed how Prof. Downing incorporates the class through 1-on-1 discussions with students. I was very engaged and look forward to the rest of the semester.

How did you feel about the cold calling?

Loved it. I hope I'm ready when I'm called.

What do you think about specifications grading?

I think that it's an interesting idea, and one that is more aligned to how we will be graded throughout our careers. I don't have too much an opinion yet, but I'm intrigued to see how it pans out.

What made you happy this week?

Being able to reconnect with my roommates who I haven't been able to see since last semester.

What's your pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

My tip of the week has to be always use assertions, and when you find a bug in your code put a assertion that can catch that bug before fixing the code! Thanks to Prof. Downing I will not forget this lesson.
